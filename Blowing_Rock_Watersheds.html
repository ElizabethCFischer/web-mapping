Headwaters of Blowing Rock, North Carolina

The town of Blowing Rock Carolina, which is just south of my hometown of Boone, North Carolina, sits along the Eastern Continental Divide, along the headwaters of three major Eastern rivers: the Catawba/Santee, the New, and the Yadkin/Pee Dee. These maps show the rough position of each of these headwaters in the town, under the names of the local creeks which eventually flow into these big rivers.

Map 1:

This is New Years Creek’s headwaters, which are by the municipal pool, just west of downtown. The creek flows to the south into the Johns River, a major tributary of the Catawba River. It joins the Catawba north of Morganton, and flows southeastward, west of Charlotte. It goes into South Carolina, passing west of Camden and east of Columbia, eventually flowing into the Atlantic north of Charleston. It undergoes several name changes along its course; it’s called the Catawba, the Wateree, and finally the Santee.

Map 2:

This is the headwaters of the Middle Fork of the South Fork of the New River (what a mouthful!). The headwaters are near the estate of Moses Cone, a robber baron who made his fortune in textiles. The river flows northward, through Boone and into Virginia, converging with the North Fork just before the state line. It runs curiously northwestward, through the crest of the Appalachians and into West Virginia, where its watershed has been made a national park. It becomes the Kanawha River and runs through central Charleston, West Virginia, and then flows into the Ohio. From there the Ohio runs on its famous course, eventually joining the Mississippi at Cairo, which flows into the Gulf of Mexico below New Orleans.

Map 3:

This map shows the headwaters of the Martin Branch, which swiftly flows into the Yadkin River. It begins right below Thunder Hill Overlook, an iconic lookout off of the Blue Ridge Parkway. The Yadkin runs lazily eastward, turning southeastward west of Winston-Salem. It flows through the rural Piedmont of North Carolina, shifting its name to the Pee Dee before crossing into South Carolina. It flows into the Atlantic at Georgetown.
